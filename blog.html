A Week in Paris: Beyond the Eiffel Tower

Paris, the city of love and lights, offers so much more than just the iconic Eiffel Tower. During my recent week-long trip, I discovered hidden gems that many tourists miss when they visit this beautiful city.

While the Eiffel Tower, Louvre, and Notre Dame are must-visit attractions, don't miss out on exploring the charming neighborhoods of Montmartre and Le Marais. Here you'll find local cafés, boutique shops, and a glimpse into the authentic Parisian lifestyle.

One of my favorite discoveries was the Sainte-Chapelle, a Gothic chapel with stunning stained glass windows that will take your breath away. Another highlight was taking a sunset boat ride along the Seine River, offering a different perspective of the city's architecture.

For food lovers, skip the touristy restaurants and venture into the side streets where you'll find family-owned bistros serving traditional French cuisine at reasonable prices. Don't forget to visit a local bakery early in the morning for freshly baked croissants – a simple but unforgettable Parisian experience.

Bali on a Budget: Experiencing Paradise Without Breaking the Bank

Bali, Indonesia's tropical paradise, has a reputation for luxury resorts and expensive experiences. However, I recently spent two weeks exploring this beautiful island on a modest budget, and I'm excited to share how you can enjoy the best of Bali without emptying your wallet.

Instead of staying in touristy areas like Kuta or Seminyak, consider accommodations in Ubud or Canggu, where you can find comfortable guesthouses for a fraction of the price. Many include breakfast and have beautiful garden or rice field views.

For food, local warungs (small family-owned restaurants) offer delicious Indonesian dishes at incredibly affordable prices. My favorite meal was Nasi Campur, a mixed plate with rice, vegetables, and various meat or tofu options that typically costs less than $3.

Transportation can be cheap too – rent a scooter for around $5 per day to explore at your own pace. Just make sure you have an international driver's license and always wear a helmet!
